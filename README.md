## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between **urban mobility indicators** (traffic congestion and travel time) and **economic performance** (GDP per capita and unemployment) across several **Latin American cities**.

The goal is to explore whether higher congestion levels are associated with measurable economic patterns.

---

## 🧩 Problem

Urban congestion is increasing in many Latin American cities, affecting mobility, productivity, and quality of life. However, the relationship between **traffic conditions and economic indicators** is not always clear.

This project investigates whether patterns exist between **traffic congestion levels and economic performance metrics**.

---

## ⚙️ What I Did

- Merged traffic and economic datasets from multiple sources
- Cleaned and standardized city-level data using **Python (Pandas)**
- Performed **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** to identify patterns
- Visualized relationships between congestion indicators and economic variables
- Prepared the data for analytical exploration in **Jupyter Notebook**
